Standing Together Against Bullying

Bullying based on appearance is the most common reason children experience bullying. It is closely tied to racism, disablism, and sexism, affecting countless young individuals worldwide (National Children’s Bureau, 2024).

In the Philippines, 7.5% of teens reported attempting suicide in 2021, up from 3% in previous years, with bullying being the leading cause. Other factors contributing to these attempts include family problems, rejection, and feelings of failure (Lauengco, 2023).

Cyberbullying further amplifies the emotional toll on victims. Research shows that 93% of cyberbullying victims experience sadness, hopelessness, and powerlessness, with cyberbullying causing greater stress than traditional bullying (Nixon, 2014; Sourander et al., 2010). Victims often report feelings of embarrassment, hurt, self-blame, and fear (Beran & Li, 2005; Price & Dalgleish, 2010).

We are committed to addressing these critical issues by promoting online kindness and empowering individuals to combat bullying in all its forms.

Cyberbullying Statistics

Understanding the Impact of Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying affects millions globally and has far-reaching consequences.
Here are some alarming statistics that highlight the prevalence of online harassment:

  • 01- Teens Affected

    72% of teens have witnessed or experienced cyberbullying firsthand.

  • 02- Mental Health Impact

    59% of victims report a negative impact on their mental health.

  • 03- Bystanders' Role

    64% of bystanders believe they can help stop online harassment.

  • 04- Students Reporting

    46% of students who experience cyberbullying never report it.

  • 05- Social Media Platforms

    80% of bullying incidents occur on social media platforms.
